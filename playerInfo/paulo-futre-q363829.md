### English Introduction
Paulo Jorge dos Santos Futre ( Portuguese pronunciation: [ˈpawlu ˈfutɾɨ] ; born 28 February 1966) is a Portuguese former professional footballer who played as a left winger .

He started his career with Sporting CP , then moved to Porto – winning the 1987 European Cup – after which he embarked in an extensive professional career, having represented clubs in Spain, France, Italy, England and Japan, most notably Atlético Madrid . He also appeared for Benfica during four months in 1993, [ 1 ] and his later years were blighted by injury problems.

A Portuguese international from the age of 17, Futre earned over 40 caps for his country, representing it at the 1986 World Cup .
